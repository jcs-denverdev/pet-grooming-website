Blog 1

I'm trying to take my game improvement seriously. This means doing more related fitness exercises, tracking my scores and each shot, consuming golf literature, and playing/practicing more often.

I got myself down to about a -4 handicap by accident a few years ago. I wasn't playing consistently well or anything. The only thing I did to bring my handicap down from a -7 was play a handful of challenging courses. Also that one day I shot even par 71 at a normal public course probably helped.

What do I see as the being the point of this blog post? I'd rather spend less time on how I used to play or how good I thought I was at one point, and more time on my current state. How are thing going with my game that I like, am I having fun? What am I hoping to get out of this? Is there a goal in mind to taking this golf improvement seriously?

Nowadays, I am unable to break 80 unless I play nine holes. What happened between 2022 and now? Is it the clubs I'm using no longer work for my aging swing speed? Is it a skill issue? I shot that 71 at a course I never played before, but I was working at GVR and had essentially unlimited access to the course and practice facilities. That certainly helps with swinging the sticks consistently, but the practice/round played had no clear intention behind them. That 71 was either luck, or it was the very top reaches of my golf talent - the absolute maximum potential I have as a golfer. Did I feel afterwards like I could have played better, like I left some shots out there? Of course. Did I celebrate after sinking that final par on 18? I don't remember, but, knowing myself, I played it humble and collected my winnings from my playing partners.
